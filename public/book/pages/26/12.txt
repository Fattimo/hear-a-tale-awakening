“Do? Nothing, except feel glad and happy to be alive.”
She was already glad and happy to be alive at the mere thought of his return.&nbsp;The murky, lowering sky, which had depressed her a few hours before, seemed&nbsp;bracing and invigorating as she splashed through the streets on her way home.
She stopped at a confectioner’s and ordered a huge box of bonbons for the&nbsp;children in Iberville. She slipped a card in the box, on which she scribbled&nbsp;a tender message and sent an abundance of kisses.
Before dinner in the evening Edna wrote a charming letter to her husband,&nbsp;telling him of her intention to move for a while into the little house around&nbsp;the block, and to give a farewell dinner before leaving, regretting that he&nbsp;was not there to share it, to help out with the menu and assist her in entertaining&nbsp;the guests.
