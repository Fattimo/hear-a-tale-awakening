It was misty, with heavy, lowering atmosphere, one afternoon, when Edna climbed&nbsp;the stairs to the pianist’s apartments under the roof. Her clothes were dripping&nbsp;with moisture. She felt chilled and pinched as she entered the room. Mademoiselle&nbsp;was poking at a rusty stove that smoked a little and warmed the room indifferently.&nbsp;She was endeavoring to heat a pot of chocolate on the stove. The room looked&nbsp;cheerless and dingy to Edna as she entered. A bust of Beethoven, covered with&nbsp;a hood of dust, scowled at her from the mantelpiece.
“Ah! here comes the sunlight!” exclaimed Mademoiselle, rising from her knees&nbsp;before the stove. “Now it will be warm and bright enough; I can let the fire&nbsp;alone.”
She closed the stove door with a bang, and approaching, assisted in removing&nbsp;Edna’s dripping mackintosh.
