Mademoiselle approached her beloved instrument and began to play. Edna did
not at once read the letter. She sat holding it in her hand, while the music
penetrated her whole being like an effulgence, warming and brightening the
dark places of her soul. It prepared her for joy and exultation.
     “Oh!” she exclaimed, letting the letter fall to the floor. “Why did you
not tell me?” She went and grasped Mademoiselle’s hands up from the keys.
“Oh! unkind! malicious! Why did you not tell me?”
     “That he was coming back? No great news, ma foi. I wonder he did not
come long ago.”
     “But when, when?” cried Edna, impatiently. “He does not say when.”
     “He says ‘very soon.’ You know as much about it as I do; it is all in
the letter.”
