Alcée Arobin wrote Edna an elaborate note of apology, palpitant with sincerity.&nbsp;It embarrassed her; for in a cooler, quieter moment it appeared to her absurd&nbsp;that she should have taken his action so seriously, so dramatically. She felt&nbsp;sure that the significance of the whole occurrence had lain in her own self-consciousness.&nbsp;If she ignored his note it would give undue importance to a trivial affair.&nbsp;If she replied to it in a serious spirit it would still leave in his mind&nbsp;the impression that she had in a susceptible moment yielded to his influence.&nbsp;After all, it was no great matter to have one’s hand kissed. She was provoked&nbsp;at his having written the apology. She answered in as light and bantering&nbsp;a spirit as she fancied it deserved, and said she would be glad to have him&nbsp;look in upon her at work whenever he felt the inclination and his business&nbsp;gave him the opportunity.
