She had been abroad with Félix, and they did not&nbsp;notice how the time was speeding. Félix is there; he will explain it all.&nbsp;He is there beside her, but she does not want to hear what he will tell her&nbsp;father.
Ma’ame Pélagie had sunk upon the bench where she and her sister so often came&nbsp;to sit. Turning, she gazed in through the gaping chasm of the window at her&nbsp;side. The interior of the ruin is ablaze. Not with the moonlight, for that&nbsp;is faint beside the other one—the sparkle from the crystal candelabra, which&nbsp;negroes, moving noiselessly and respectfully about, are lighting, one after&nbsp;the other. How the gleam of them reflects and glances from the polished marble&nbsp;pillars!
