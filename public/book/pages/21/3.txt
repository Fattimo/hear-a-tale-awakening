“Yes,” she went on; “I sometimes thought: ‘She will never come. She promised as
those women in society always do, without meaning it. She will not come.’ For I
really don’t believe you like me, Mrs. Pontellier.”
“I don’t know whether I like you or not,” replied Edna, gazing down at the little
woman with a quizzical look.
The candor of Mrs. Pontellier’s admission greatly pleased Mademoiselle Reisz. She
