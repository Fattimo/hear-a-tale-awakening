Edna was sobbing, just as she had wept one midnight at Grand Isle when strange,&nbsp;new voices awoke in her. She arose in some agitation to take her departure.&nbsp;“May I come again, Mademoiselle?” she asked at the threshold.
“Come whenever you feel like it. Be careful; the stairs and landings are dark;&nbsp;don’t stumble.”
Mademoiselle reentered and lit a candle. Robert’s letter was on the floor.&nbsp;She stooped and picked it up. It was crumpled and damp with tears. Mademoiselle&nbsp;smoothed the letter out, restored it to the envelope, and replaced it in the&nbsp;table drawer.
