Mademoiselle played a soft interlude. It was an improvisation. She sat low&nbsp;at the instrument, and the lines of her body settled into ungraceful curves&nbsp;and angles that gave it an appearance of deformity. Gradually and imperceptibly&nbsp;the interlude melted into the soft opening minor chords of the Chopin Impromptu.
Edna did not know when the Impromptu began or ended. She sat in the sofa corner&nbsp;reading Robert’s letter by the fading light. Mademoiselle had glided from&nbsp;the Chopin into the quivering love notes of Isolde’s song, and back again&nbsp;to the Impromptu with its soulful and poignant longing.
