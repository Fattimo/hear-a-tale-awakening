“I said all along I was going to Mexico; I’ve been saying so for years!” cried&nbsp;Robert, in an excited and irritable tone, with the air of a man defending&nbsp;himself against a swarm of stinging insects.
Madame Lebrun knocked on the table with her knife handle.
“Please let Robert explain why he is going, and why he is going to-night,”&nbsp;she called out. “Really, this table is getting to be more and more like Bedlam&nbsp;every day, with everybody talking at once. Sometimes—I hope God will forgive&nbsp;me—but positively, sometimes I wish Victor would lose the power of speech.”
Victor laughed sardonically as he thanked his mother for her holy wish, of&nbsp;which he failed to see the benefit to anybody, except that it might afford&nbsp;her a more ample opportunity and license to talk herself.
