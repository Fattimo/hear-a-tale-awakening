His family&nbsp;lived in commodious apartments over the store, having an entrance on the side&nbsp;within the porte cochère. There was something which Edna thought very French,&nbsp;very foreign, about their whole manner of living. In the large and pleasant&nbsp;salon which extended across the width of the house, the Ratignolles entertained&nbsp;their friends once a fortnight with a soirée musicale, sometimes diversified&nbsp;by card-playing. There was a friend who played upon the cello. One brought&nbsp;his flute and another his violin, while there were some who sang and a number&nbsp;who performed upon the piano with various degrees of taste and agility. The&nbsp;Ratignolles’ soirées musicales were widely known, and it was considered a&nbsp;privilege to be invited to them.
