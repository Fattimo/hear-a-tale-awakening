“Perhaps I shall be able to paint your picture some day,” said Edna with a&nbsp;smile when they were seated. She produced the roll of sketches and started&nbsp;to unfold them. “I believe I ought to work again. I feel as if I wanted to&nbsp;be doing something. What do you think of them? Do you think it worth while&nbsp;to take it up again and study some more? I might study for a while with Laidpore.”
She knew that Madame Ratignolle’s opinion in such a matter would be next to&nbsp;valueless, that she herself had not alone decided, but determined; but she&nbsp;sought the words of praise and encouragement that would help her to put heart&nbsp;into her venture.
“Your talent is immense, dear!”
“Nonsense!” protested Edna, well pleased.
