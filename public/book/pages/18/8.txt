One might almost be tempted to reach out a hand and take one.”
Edna could not control a feeling which bordered upon complacency at her friend’s&nbsp;praise, even realizing, as she did, its true worth. She retained a few of&nbsp;the sketches, and gave all the rest to Madame Ratignolle, who appreciated&nbsp;the gift far beyond its value and proudly exhibited the pictures to her husband&nbsp;when he came up from the store a little later for his midday dinner.
