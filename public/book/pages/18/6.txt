“’Cité can do it as well as I; it is really her business,” she explained to&nbsp;Edna, who apologized for interrupting her. And she summoned a young black&nbsp;woman, whom she instructed, in French, to be very careful in checking off&nbsp;the list which she handed her. She told her to notice particularly if a fine&nbsp;linen handkerchief of Monsieur Ratignolle’s, which was missing last week,&nbsp;had been returned; and to be sure to set to one side such pieces as required&nbsp;mending and darning.
Then placing an arm around Edna’s waist, she led her to the front of the house,&nbsp;to the salon, where it was cool and sweet with the odor of great roses that&nbsp;stood upon the hearth in jars.
