The following morning Mr. Pontellier, upon leaving for his office, asked Edna&nbsp;if she would not meet him in town in order to look at some new fixtures for&nbsp;the library.
“I hardly think we need new fixtures, Léonce. Don’t let us get anything new;&nbsp;you are too extravagant. I don’t believe you ever think of saving or putting&nbsp;by.”
“The way to become rich is to make money, my dear Edna, not to save it,” he&nbsp;said. He regretted that she did not feel inclined to go with him and select&nbsp;new fixtures. He kissed her good-by, and told her she was not looking well&nbsp;and must take care of herself. She was unusually pale and very quiet.
