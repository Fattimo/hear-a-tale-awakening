The Ratignolles understood each other perfectly. If ever&nbsp;the fusion of two human beings into one has been accomplished on this sphere&nbsp;it was surely in their union.
As Edna seated herself at table with them she thought, “Better a dinner of&nbsp;herbs,” though it did not take her long to discover that it was no dinner&nbsp;of herbs, but a delicious repast, simple, choice, and in every way satisfying.
Monsieur Ratignolle was delighted to see her, though he found her looking&nbsp;not so well as at Grand Isle, and he advised a tonic.
