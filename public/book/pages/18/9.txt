     As Edna seated herself at table with them she thought, “Better a dinner
of herbs,” though it did not take her long to discover that it was no dinner
of herbs, but a delicious repast, simple, choice, and in every way satisfying.

     Monsieur Ratignolle was delighted to see her, though he found her looking
not so well as at Grand Isle, and he advised a tonic. He talked a good deal
on various topics, a little politics, some city news and neighborhood gossip.
