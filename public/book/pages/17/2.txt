On Tuesday afternoons—Tuesday being Mrs. Pontellier’s reception day—there&nbsp;was a constant stream of callers—women who came in carriages or in the street&nbsp;cars, or walked when the air was soft and distance permitted. A light-colored&nbsp;mulatto boy, in dress coat and bearing a diminutive silver tray for the reception&nbsp;of cards, admitted them. A maid, in white fluted cap, offered the callers&nbsp;liqueur, coffee, or chocolate, as they might desire.
