“It seems to me,” he said, “we spend money enough in this house to procure&nbsp;at least one meal a day which a man could eat and retain his self-respect.”
“You used to think the cook was a treasure,” returned Edna, indifferently.
“Perhaps she was when she first came; but cooks are only human. They need&nbsp;looking after, like any other class of persons that you employ. Suppose I&nbsp;didn’t look after the clerks in my office, just let them run things their&nbsp;own way; they’d soon make a nice mess of me and my business.”
“Where are you going?” asked Edna, seeing that her husband arose from table&nbsp;without having eaten a morsel except a taste of the highly-seasoned soup.
