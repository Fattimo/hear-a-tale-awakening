All the mystery and witchery of the night seemed&nbsp;to have gathered there amid the perfumes and the dusky and tortuous outlines&nbsp;of flowers and foliage. She was seeking herself and finding herself in just&nbsp;such sweet, half-darkness which met her moods. But the voices were not soothing&nbsp;that came to her from the darkness and the sky above and the stars. They jeered&nbsp;and sounded mournful notes without promise, devoid even of hope. She turned&nbsp;back into the room and began to walk to and fro down its whole length without&nbsp;stopping, without resting. She carried in her hands a thin handkerchief, which&nbsp;she tore into ribbons, rolled into a ball, and flung from her.
