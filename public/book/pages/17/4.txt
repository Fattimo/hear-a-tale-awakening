“Tired out, Edna? Whom did you have? Many callers?” he asked. He tasted his&nbsp;soup and began to season it with pepper, salt, vinegar, mustard—everything&nbsp;within reach.
“There were a good many,” replied Edna, who was eating her soup with evident&nbsp;satisfaction. “I found their cards when I got home; I was out.”
“Out!” exclaimed her husband, with something like genuine consternation in&nbsp;his voice as he laid down the vinegar cruet and looked at her through his&nbsp;glasses. “Why, what could have taken you out on Tuesday? What did you have&nbsp;to do?”
“Nothing. I simply felt like going out, and I went out.”
“Well, I hope you left some suitable excuse,” said her husband, somewhat appeased,&nbsp;as he added a dash of cayenne pepper to the soup.
“No, I left no excuse. I told Joe to say I was out, that was all.”
