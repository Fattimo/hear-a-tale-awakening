She hoped to remain unnoticed, but her white gown revealed&nbsp;her to him. He threw away his cigar and seated himself upon the bench beside&nbsp;her; without a suspicion that she might object to his presence.
“Your husband told me to bring this to you, Mrs. Baroda,” he said, handing&nbsp;her a filmy, white scarf with which she sometimes enveloped her head and shoulders.&nbsp;She accepted the scarf from him with a murmur of thanks, and let it lie in&nbsp;her lap.
He made some commonplace observation upon the baneful effect of the night&nbsp;air at the season. Then as his gaze reached out into the darkness, he murmured,&nbsp;half to himself:
“‘Night of south winds—night of the large few stars!
Still nodding night—’”
