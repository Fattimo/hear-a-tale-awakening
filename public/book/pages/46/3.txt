“This is what I call living,” he would utter with deep satisfaction, as the&nbsp;air that swept across the sugar field caressed him with its warm and scented&nbsp;velvety touch. It pleased him also to get on familiar terms with the big dogs&nbsp;that came about him, rubbing themselves sociably against his legs. He did&nbsp;not care to fish, and displayed no eagerness to go out and kill grosbecs when&nbsp;Gaston proposed doing so.
Gouvernail’s personality puzzled Mrs. Baroda, but she liked him. Indeed, he&nbsp;was a lovable, inoffensive fellow. After a few days, when she could understand&nbsp;him no better than at first, she gave over being puzzled and remained piqued.&nbsp;In this mood she left her husband and her guest, for the most part, alone&nbsp;together.
