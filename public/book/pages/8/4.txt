“You made one mistake, Adèle,” he said, with a light smile; “there is no earthly&nbsp;possibility of Mrs. Pontellier ever taking me seriously. You should have warned&nbsp;me against taking myself seriously. Your advice might then have carried some&nbsp;weight and given me subject for some reflection. Au revoir. But you look tired,”&nbsp;he added, solicitously. “Would you like a cup of bouillon? Shall I stir you&nbsp;a toddy? Let me mix you a toddy with a drop of Angostura.”
