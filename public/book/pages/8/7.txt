“Where is Victor going with the rockaway?”
“The rockaway? Victor?”
“Yes; down there in front. He seems to be getting ready to drive away somewhere.”
“Call him.” Clatter, clatter!
Robert uttered a shrill, piercing whistle which might have been heard back&nbsp;at the wharf.
“He won’t look up.”
Madame Lebrun flew to the window. She called “Victor!” She waved a handkerchief&nbsp;and called again. The young fellow below got into the vehicle and started&nbsp;the horse off at a gallop.
Madame Lebrun went back to the machine, crimson with annoyance. Victor was&nbsp;the younger son and brother—a tête montée, with a temper which invited violence&nbsp;and a will which no ax could break.
