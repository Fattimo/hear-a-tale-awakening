Madame Lebrun was busily engaged at the sewing-machine. A little black girl&nbsp;sat on the floor, and with her hands worked the treadle of the machine. The&nbsp;Creole woman does not take any chances which may be avoided of imperiling&nbsp;her health.
Robert went over and seated himself on the broad sill of one of the dormer&nbsp;windows. He took a book from his pocket and began energetically to read it,&nbsp;judging by the precision and frequency with which he turned the leaves. The&nbsp;sewing-machine made a resounding clatter in the room; it was of a ponderous,&nbsp;by-gone make. In the lulls, Robert and his mother exchanged bits of desultory&nbsp;conversation.
