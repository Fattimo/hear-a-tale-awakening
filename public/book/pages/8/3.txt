     “Oh! well! That isn’t it,” slamming his hat down vehemently upon his
head. “You ought to feel that such things are not flattering to say to a fellow.”

     “Should our whole intercourse consist of an exchange of compliments? Ma
foi!”

     “It isn’t pleasant to have a woman tell you—” he went on, unheedingly,
but breaking off suddenly: “Now if I were like Arobin—you remember Alcée Arobin
and that story of the consul’s wife at Biloxi?” And he related the story of
Alcée Arobin and the consul’s wife; and another about the tenor of the French
Opera, who received letters which should never have been written; and still
other stories, grave and gay, till Mrs. Pontellier and her possible propensity
for taking young men seriously was apparently forgotten.
