Much to her surprise she received a note from Arobin, written at midnight&nbsp;from the club. It was to say good morning to her, to hope she had slept well,&nbsp;to assure her of his devotion, which he trusted she in some faintest manner&nbsp;returned.
All these letters were pleasing to her. She answered the children in a cheerful&nbsp;frame of mind, promising them bonbons, and congratulating them upon their&nbsp;happy find of the little pigs.
She answered her husband with friendly evasiveness,—not with any fixed design&nbsp;to mislead him, only because all sense of reality had gone out of her life;&nbsp;she had abandoned herself to Fate, and awaited the consequences with indifference.
To Arobin’s note she made no reply. She put it under Celestine’s stove-lid.
