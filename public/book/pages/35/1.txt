The morning was full of sunlight and hope. Edna could see before her no denial—only&nbsp;the promise of excessive joy. She lay in bed awake, with bright eyes full&nbsp;of speculation. “He loves you, poor fool.” If she could but get that conviction&nbsp;firmly fixed in her mind, what mattered about the rest? She felt she had been&nbsp;childish and unwise the night before in giving herself over to despondency.&nbsp;She recapitulated the motives which no doubt explained Robert’s reserve. They&nbsp;were not insurmountable; they would not hold if he really loved her; they&nbsp;could not hold against her own passion, which he must come to realize in time.&nbsp;She pictured him going to his business that morning.
