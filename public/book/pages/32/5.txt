     She went with them herself to see the pigs and the cows, to look at the
darkies laying the cane, to thrash the pecan trees, and catch fish in the
back lake. She lived with them a whole week long, giving them all of herself,
and gathering and filling herself with their young existence. They listened,
breathless, when she told them the house in Esplanade Street was crowded with
workmen, hammering, nailing, sawing, and filling the place with clatter. They
wanted to know where their bed was; what had been done with their rocking-horse;
and where did Joe sleep, and where had Ellen gone, and the cook? But, above
