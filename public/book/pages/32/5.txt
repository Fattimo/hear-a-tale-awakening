She went with them herself to see the pigs and the cows, to look at the darkies&nbsp;laying the cane, to thrash the pecan trees, and catch fish in the back lake.&nbsp;She lived with them a whole week long, giving them all of herself, and gathering&nbsp;and filling herself with their young existence. They listened, breathless,&nbsp;when she told them the house in Esplanade Street was crowded with workmen,&nbsp;hammering, nailing, sawing, and filling the place with clatter. They wanted&nbsp;to know where their bed was; what had been done with their rocking-horse;&nbsp;and where did Joe sleep, and where had Ellen gone, and the cook?
