“Who gave it to you?” she laughed.
“I bought it. I suppose I’m getting reckless; I bought a whole box.” She was
determined not to be personal again and make him uncomfortable.
The cat made friends with him, and climbed into his lap when he smoked his cigar.
He stroked her silky fur, and talked a little about her. He looked at Edna’s book,
which he had read; and he told her the end, to save her the trouble of wading
through it, he said.
Again he accompanied her back to her home; and it was after dusk when they
reached the little “pigeon-house.” She did not ask him to remain, which he was
grateful for, as it permitted him to stay without the discomfort of blundering
through an excuse which he had no intention of considering. He helped her to
light the lamp; then she went into her room to take off her hat and to bathe her face
and hands.
