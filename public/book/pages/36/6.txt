“Who gave it to you?” she laughed.
“I bought it. I suppose I’m getting reckless; I bought a whole box.” She was&nbsp;determined not to be personal again and make him uncomfortable.
The cat made friends with him, and climbed into his lap when he smoked his&nbsp;cigar. He stroked her silky fur, and talked a little about her. He looked&nbsp;at Edna’s book, which he had read; and he told her the end, to save her the&nbsp;trouble of wading through it, he said.
Again he accompanied her back to her home; and it was after dusk when they&nbsp;reached the little “pigeon-house.” She did not ask him to remain, which he&nbsp;was grateful for, as it permitted him to stay without the discomfort of blundering&nbsp;through an excuse which he had no intention of considering. He helped her&nbsp;to light the lamp; then she went into her room to take off her hat and to&nbsp;bathe her face and hands.
