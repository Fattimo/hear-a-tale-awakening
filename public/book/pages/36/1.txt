There was a garden out in the suburbs; a small, leafy corner, with a few green&nbsp;tables under the orange trees. An old cat slept all day on the stone step&nbsp;in the sun, and an old mulatresse slept her idle hours away in her chair at&nbsp;the open window, till some one happened to knock on one of the green tables.&nbsp;She had milk and cream cheese to sell, and bread and butter. There was no&nbsp;one who could make such excellent coffee or fry a chicken so golden brown&nbsp;as she.
The place was too modest to attract the attention of people of fashion, and&nbsp;so quiet as to have escaped the notice of those in search of pleasure and&nbsp;dissipation. Edna had discovered it accidentally one day when the high-board&nbsp;gate stood ajar.
