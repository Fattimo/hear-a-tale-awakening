the shadow, leaning his head back on the chair as if in a reverie. Edna lingered a
moment beside the table, arranging the books there. Then she went across the
room to where he sat. She bent over the arm of his chair and called his name.
“Robert,” she said, “are you asleep?”
“No,” he answered, looking up at her.
She leaned over and kissed him—a soft, cool, delicate kiss, whose voluptuous sting
penetrated his whole being—then she moved away from him. He followed, and
took her in his arms, just holding her close to him. She put her hand up to his face
and pressed his cheek against her own. The action was full of love and tenderness.
He sought her lips again. Then he drew her down upon the sofa beside him and
held her hand in both of his.
