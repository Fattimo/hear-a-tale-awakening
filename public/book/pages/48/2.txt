She had seen some beautiful patterns, veritable bargains in the shop&nbsp;windows. And still there would be left enough for new stockings—two pairs&nbsp;apiece—and what darning that would save for a while! She would get caps for&nbsp;the boys and sailor-hats for the girls. The vision of her little brood looking&nbsp;fresh and dainty and new for once in their lives excited her and made her&nbsp;restless and wakeful with anticipation.
The neighbors sometimes talked of certain “better days” that little Mrs. Sommers&nbsp;had known before she had ever thought of being Mrs. Sommers.
