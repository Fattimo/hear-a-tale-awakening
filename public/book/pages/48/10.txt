While waiting to be served she removed her gloves very leisurely and laid&nbsp;them beside her. Then she picked up a magazine and glanced through it, cutting&nbsp;the pages with a blunt edge of her knife. It was all very agreeable. The damask&nbsp;was even more spotless than it had seemed through the window, and the crystal&nbsp;more sparkling. There were quiet ladies and gentlemen, who did not notice&nbsp;her, lunching at the small tables like her own. A soft, pleasing strain of&nbsp;music could be heard, and a gentle breeze was blowing through the window.&nbsp;She tasted a bite, and she read a word or two, and she sipped the amber wine&nbsp;and wiggled her toes in the silk stockings. The price of it made no difference.&nbsp;She counted the money out to the waiter and left an extra coin on his tray,&nbsp;whereupon he bowed before her as before a princess of royal blood.
