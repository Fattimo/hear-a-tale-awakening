She was fastidious. The clerk could not make her out; he could not reconcile&nbsp;her shoes with her stockings, and she was not too easily pleased. She held&nbsp;back her skirts and turned her feet one way and her head another way as she&nbsp;glanced down at the polished, pointed-tipped boots. Her foot and ankle looked&nbsp;very pretty. She could not realize that they belonged to her and were a part&nbsp;of herself. She wanted an excellent and stylish fit, she told the young fellow&nbsp;who served her, and she did not mind the difference of a dollar or two more&nbsp;in the price so long as she got what she desired.
It was a long time since Mrs. Sommers had been fitted with gloves. On rare&nbsp;occasions when she had bought a pair they were always “bargains,” so cheap&nbsp;that it would have been preposterous and unreasonable to have expected them&nbsp;to be fitted to the hand.
