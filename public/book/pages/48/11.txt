It was a little later when she entered the theatre, the play had begun and&nbsp;the house seemed to her to be packed. But there were vacant seats here and&nbsp;there, and into one of them she was ushered, between brilliantly dressed women&nbsp;who had gone there to kill time and eat candy and display their gaudy attire.&nbsp;There were many others who were there solely for the play and acting. It is&nbsp;safe to say there was no one present who bore quite the attitude which Mrs.&nbsp;Sommers did to her surroundings. She gathered in the whole—stage and players&nbsp;and people in one wide impression, and absorbed it and enjoyed it. She laughed&nbsp;at the comedy and wept—she and the gaudy woman next to her wept over the tragedy.&nbsp;And they talked a little together over it.
