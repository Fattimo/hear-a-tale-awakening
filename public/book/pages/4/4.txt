She had brought the pattern of the drawers for Mrs. Pontellier to cut out—a&nbsp;marvel of construction, fashioned to enclose a baby’s body so effectually&nbsp;that only two small eyes might look out from the garment, like an Eskimo’s.&nbsp;They were designed for winter wear, when treacherous drafts came down chimneys&nbsp;and insidious currents of deadly cold found their way through key-holes.
Mrs. Pontellier’s mind was quite at rest concerning the present material needs&nbsp;of her children, and she could not see the use of anticipating and making&nbsp;winter night garments the subject of her summer meditations.
