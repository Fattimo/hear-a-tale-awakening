Despondency had come upon her there in the wakeful night, and had never lifted.&nbsp;There was no one thing in the world that she desired. There was no human being&nbsp;whom she wanted near her except Robert; and she even realized that the day&nbsp;would come when he, too, and the thought of him would melt out of her existence,&nbsp;leaving her alone. The children appeared before her like antagonists who had&nbsp;overcome her; who had overpowered and sought to drag her into the soul’s slavery&nbsp;for the rest of her days.
