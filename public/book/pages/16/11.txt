But Mademoiselle waited. She was very amiable during the walk back, and raved&nbsp;much over Edna’s appearance in her bathing suit. She talked about music. She&nbsp;hoped that Edna would go to see her in the city, and wrote her address with&nbsp;the stub of a pencil on a piece of card which she found in her pocket.
“When do you leave?” asked Edna.
“Next Monday; and you?”
“The following week,” answered Edna, adding, “It has been a pleasant summer,&nbsp;hasn’t it, Mademoiselle?”
“Well,” agreed Mademoiselle Reisz, with a shrug, “rather pleasant, if it hadn’t&nbsp;been for the mosquitoes and the Farival twins.”
