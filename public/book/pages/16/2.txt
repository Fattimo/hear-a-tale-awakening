She went up in the&nbsp;mornings to Madame Lebrun’s room, braving the clatter of the old sewing-machine.&nbsp;She sat there and chatted at intervals as Robert had done. She gazed around&nbsp;the room at the pictures and photographs hanging upon the wall, and discovered&nbsp;in some corner an old family album, which she examined with the keenest interest,&nbsp;appealing to Madame Lebrun for enlightenment concerning the many figures and&nbsp;faces which she discovered between its pages.
There was a picture of Madame Lebrun with Robert as a baby, seated in her&nbsp;lap, a round-faced infant with a fist in his mouth. The eyes alone in the&nbsp;baby suggested the man. And that was he also in kilts, at the age of five,&nbsp;wearing long curls and holding a whip in his hand.
