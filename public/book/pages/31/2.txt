When everything was secured and the lights put out, they left through the&nbsp;front door, Arobin locking it and taking the key, which he carried for Edna.&nbsp;He helped her down the steps.
“Will you have a spray of jessamine?” he asked, breaking off a few blossoms&nbsp;as he passed.
“No; I don’t want anything.”
She seemed disheartened, and had nothing to say. She took his arm, which he&nbsp;offered her, holding up the weight of her satin train with the other hand.&nbsp;She looked down, noticing the black line of his leg moving in and out so close&nbsp;to her against the yellow shimmer of her gown. There was the whistle of a&nbsp;railway train somewhere in the distance, and the midnight bells were ringing.&nbsp;They met no one in their short walk.
