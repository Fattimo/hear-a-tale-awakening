It would be pitiful to tell how their days were spent to accomplish this end;&nbsp;how the dollars had been saved for thirty years and the picayunes hoarded;&nbsp;and yet, not half enough gathered! But Ma’ame Pélagie felt sure of twenty&nbsp;years of life before her, and counted upon as many more for her sister. And&nbsp;what could not come to pass in twenty—in forty—years?
