The girl was quite as tall as her aunt Pélagie, with dark eyes&nbspthat reflected joy as a still pool reflects the light of stars; and her rounded&nbspcheek was tinged like the pink crèpe myrtle. Mam’selle Pauline kissed her&nbspand trembled. Ma’ame Pélagie looked into her eyes with a searching gaze, which&nbspseemed to seek a likeness of the past in the living present.
