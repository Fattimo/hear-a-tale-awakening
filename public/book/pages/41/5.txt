Then Ma’ame Pélagie rose with stately deliberation and went to saddle her&nbsp;horse, for she had yet to make her last daily round through the fields; and&nbsp;Mam’selle Pauline threaded her way slowly among the tangled grasses toward&nbsp;the cabin.
The coming of La Petite, bringing with her as she did the pungent atmosphere&nbsp;of an outside and dimly known world, was a shock to these two, living their&nbsp;dream-life. The girl was quite as tall as her aunt Pélagie, with dark eyes&nbsp;that reflected joy as a still pool reflects the light of stars; and her rounded&nbsp;cheek was tinged like the pink crèpe myrtle. Mam’selle Pauline kissed her&nbsp;and trembled. Ma’ame Pélagie looked into her eyes with a searching gaze, which&nbsp;seemed to seek a likeness of the past in the living present.
And they made room between them for this young life.
