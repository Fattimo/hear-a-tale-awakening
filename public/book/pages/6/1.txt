Edna Pontellier could not have told why, wishing to go to the beach with Robert,&nbsp;she should in the first place have declined, and in the second place have&nbsp;followed in obedience to one of the two contradictory impulses which impelled&nbsp;her.
A certain light was beginning to dawn dimly within her,—the light which, showing&nbsp;the way, forbids it.
At that early period it served but to bewilder her. It moved her to dreams,&nbsp;to thoughtfulness, to the shadowy anguish which had overcome her the midnight&nbsp;when she had abandoned herself to tears.
In short, Mrs. Pontellier was beginning to realize her position in the universe&nbsp;as a human being, and to recognize her relations as an individual to the world&nbsp;within and about her.
