He stayed and dined with Edna. He stayed and sat beside the wood fire. They&nbsp;laughed and talked; and before it was time to go he was telling her how different&nbsp;life might have been if he had known her years before. With ingenuous frankness&nbsp;he spoke of what a wicked, ill-disciplined boy he had been, and impulsively&nbsp;drew up his cuff to exhibit upon his wrist the scar from a saber cut which&nbsp;he had received in a duel outside of Paris when he was nineteen. She touched&nbsp;his hand as she scanned the red cicatrice on the inside of his white wrist.&nbsp;A quick impulse that was somewhat spasmodic impelled her fingers to close&nbsp;in a sort of clutch upon his hand.
