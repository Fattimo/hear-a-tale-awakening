She did not mean her husband; she was thinking of Robert Lebrun. Her husband&nbsp;seemed to her now like a person whom she had married without love as an excuse.
She lit a candle and went up to her room. Alcée Arobin was absolutely nothing&nbsp;to her. Yet his presence, his manners, the warmth of his glances, and above&nbsp;all the touch of his lips upon her hand had acted like a narcotic upon her.
She slept a languorous sleep, interwoven with vanishing dreams.
