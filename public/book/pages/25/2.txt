Mrs. Highcamp&nbsp;was a worldly but unaffected, intelligent, slim, tall blonde woman in the&nbsp;forties, with an indifferent manner and blue eyes that stared. She had a daughter&nbsp;who served her as a pretext for cultivating the society of young men of fashion.&nbsp;Alcée Arobin was one of them. He was a familiar figure at the race course,&nbsp;the opera, the fashionable clubs. There was a perpetual smile in his eyes,&nbsp;which seldom failed to awaken a corresponding cheerfulness in any one who&nbsp;looked into them and listened to his good-humored voice. His manner was quiet,&nbsp;and at times a little insolent. He possessed a good figure, a pleasing face,&nbsp;not overburdened with depth of thought or feeling; and his dress was that&nbsp;of the conventional man of fashion.
