When the weather was dark and cloudy Edna could not work. She needed the sun&nbsp;to mellow and temper her mood to the sticking point. She had reached a stage&nbsp;when she seemed to be no longer feeling her way, working, when in the humor,&nbsp;with sureness and ease. And being devoid of ambition, and striving not toward&nbsp;accomplishment, she drew satisfaction from the work in itself.
On rainy or melancholy days Edna went out and sought the society of the friends&nbsp;she had made at Grand Isle. Or else she stayed indoors and nursed a mood with&nbsp;which she was becoming too familiar for her own comfort and peace of mind.&nbsp;It was not despair; but it seemed to her as if life were passing by, leaving&nbsp;its promise broken and unfulfilled. Yet there were other days when she listened,&nbsp;was led on and deceived by fresh promises which her youth held out to her.
