There were possibly a few track men out there who knew the race horse as well&nbsp;as Edna, but there was certainly none who knew it better. She sat between&nbsp;her two companions as one having authority to speak. She laughed at Arobin’s&nbsp;pretensions, and deplored Mrs. Highcamp’s ignorance. The race horse was a&nbsp;friend and intimate associate of her childhood. The atmosphere of the stables&nbsp;and the breath of the blue grass paddock revived in her memory and lingered&nbsp;in her nostrils. She did not perceive that she was talking like her father&nbsp;as the sleek geldings ambled in review before them. She played for very high&nbsp;stakes, and fortune favored her.
