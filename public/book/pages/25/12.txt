“Your manner has not misled me, Mrs. Pontellier,” he said finally. “My own&nbsp;emotions have done that. I couldn’t help it. When I’m near you, how could&nbsp;I help it? Don’t think anything of it, don’t bother, please. You see, I go&nbsp;when you command me. If you wish me to stay away, I shall do so. If you let&nbsp;me come back, I—oh! you will let me come back?”
He cast one appealing glance at her, to which she made no response. Alcée&nbsp;Arobin’s manner was so genuine that it often deceived even himself.
Edna did not care or think whether it were genuine or not. When she was alone&nbsp;she looked mechanically at the back of her hand which he had kissed so warmly.&nbsp;Then she leaned her head down on the mantelpiece. She felt somewhat like a&nbsp;woman who in a moment of passion is betrayed into an act of infidelity, and&nbsp;realizes the significance of the act without being wholly awakened from its&nbsp;glamour.
