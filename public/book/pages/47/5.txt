understand that Mr. Harvy is an intimate friend of long standing. Why, we
have always been like cousins—like brother and sister, I may say. He is my
brother’s most intimate associate and often fancies that he is entitled to
the same privileges as the family. Oh, I know it is absurd, uncalled for,
to tell you this; undignified even,” she was almost weeping, “but it makes
so much difference to me what you think of—of me.” Her voice had grown very
low and agitated. The misery had all disappeared from Brantain’s face.

     “Then you do really care what I think, Miss Nathalie? May I call you
Miss Nathalie?” They turned into a long, dim corridor that was lined on either
side with tall, graceful plants. They walked slowly to the very end of it.
