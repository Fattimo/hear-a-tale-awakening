She scolded&nbsp;him, laughed at him, played with him. The garden smelled so good and looked&nbsp;so pretty in the afternoon sunlight. Edna plucked all the bright flowers she&nbsp;could find, and went into the house with them, she and the little dog.
Even the kitchen assumed a sudden interesting character which she had never&nbsp;before perceived. She went in to give directions to the cook, to say that&nbsp;the butcher would have to bring much less meat, that they would require only&nbsp;half their usual quantity of bread, of milk and groceries. She told the cook&nbsp;that she herself would be greatly occupied during Mr. Pontellier’s absence,&nbsp;and she begged her to take all thought and responsibility of the larder upon&nbsp;her own shoulders.
