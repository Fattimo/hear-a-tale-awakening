When Edna was at last alone, she breathed a big, genuine sigh of relief. A&nbsp;feeling that was unfamiliar but very delicious came over her. She walked all&nbsp;through the house, from one room to another, as if inspecting it for the first&nbsp;time. She tried the various chairs and lounges, as if she had never sat and&nbsp;reclined upon them before. And she perambulated around the outside of the&nbsp;house, investigating, looking to see if windows and shutters were secure and&nbsp;in order. The flowers were like new acquaintances; she approached them in&nbsp;a familiar spirit, and made herself at home among them.
