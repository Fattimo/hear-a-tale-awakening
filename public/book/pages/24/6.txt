The cook, placed upon her mettle, served a delicious&nbsp;repast—a luscious tenderloin broiled à point. The wine tasted good; the marron&nbsp;glacé seemed to be just what she wanted. It was so pleasant, too, to dine&nbsp;in a comfortable peignoir.
She thought a little sentimentally about Léonce and the children, and wondered&nbsp;what they were doing. As she gave a dainty scrap or two to the doggie, she&nbsp;talked intimately to him about Etienne and Raoul. He was beside himself with&nbsp;astonishment and delight over these companionable advances, and showed his&nbsp;appreciation by his little quick, snappy barks and a lively agitation.
