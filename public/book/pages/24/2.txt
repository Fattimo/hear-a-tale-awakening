     “You are too lenient, too lenient by far, Léonce,” asserted the Colonel.
“Authority, coercion are what is needed. Put your foot down good and hard;
the only way to manage a wife. Take my word for it.”
     The Colonel was perhaps unaware that he had coerced his own wife into
her grave. Mr. Pontellier had a vague suspicion of it which he thought it
needless to mention at that late day.
     Edna was not so consciously gratified at her husband’s leaving home as
she had been over the departure of her father. As the day approached when
he was to leave her for a comparatively long stay, she grew melting and affectionate,
remembering his many acts of consideration and his repeated expressions of
