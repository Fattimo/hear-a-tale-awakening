“You are too lenient, too lenient by far, Léonce,” asserted the Colonel. “Authority,&nbsp;coercion are what is needed. Put your foot down good and hard; the only way&nbsp;to manage a wife. Take my word for it.”
The Colonel was perhaps unaware that he had coerced his own wife into her&nbsp;grave. Mr. Pontellier had a vague suspicion of it which he thought it needless&nbsp;to mention at that late day.
Edna was not so consciously gratified at her husband’s leaving home as she&nbsp;had been over the departure of her father. As the day approached when he was&nbsp;to leave her for a comparatively long stay, she grew melting and affectionate,&nbsp;remembering his many acts of consideration and his repeated expressions of&nbsp;an ardent attachment.
