Victor was surprised and delighted to see Mrs. Pontellier, and he made no&nbsp;attempt to conceal either his astonishment or his delight. He was a dark-browed,&nbsp;good-looking youngster of nineteen, greatly resembling his mother, but with&nbsp;ten times her impetuosity. He instructed the black woman to go at once and&nbsp;inform Madame Lebrun that Mrs. Pontellier desired to see her. The woman grumbled&nbsp;a refusal to do part of her duty when she had not been permitted to do it&nbsp;all, and started back to her interrupted task of weeding the garden.
