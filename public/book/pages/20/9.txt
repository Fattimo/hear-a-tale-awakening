The despondent frame of mind in which she had left home began again to&nbsp;overtake her, and she remembered that she wished to find Mademoiselle Reisz.
Madame Lebrun knew where Mademoiselle Reisz lived. She gave Edna the address,&nbsp;regretting that she would not consent to stay and spend the remainder of the&nbsp;afternoon, and pay a visit to Mademoiselle Reisz some other day. The afternoon&nbsp;was already well advanced.
Victor escorted her out upon the banquette, lifted her parasol, and held it&nbsp;over her while he walked to the car with her. He entreated her to bear in&nbsp;mind that the disclosures of the afternoon were strictly confidential.
