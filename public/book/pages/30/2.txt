Monsieur Ratignolle brought himself and his wife’s excuses.&nbsp;Victor Lebrun, who happened to be in the city, bent upon relaxation, had accepted&nbsp;with alacrity. There was a Miss Mayblunt, no longer in her teens, who looked&nbsp;at the world through lorgnettes and with the keenest interest. It was thought&nbsp;and said that she was intellectual; it was suspected of her that she wrote&nbsp;under a nom de guerre. She had come with a gentleman by the name of Gouvernail,&nbsp;connected with one of the daily papers, of whom nothing special could be said,&nbsp;except that he was observant and seemed quiet and inoffensive. Edna herself&nbsp;made the tenth, and at half-past eight they seated themselves at table, Arobin&nbsp;and Monsieur Ratignolle on either side of their hostess.
