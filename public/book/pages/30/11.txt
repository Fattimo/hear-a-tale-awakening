She took from&nbsp;the back of her chair a white silken scarf, with which she had covered her&nbsp;shoulders in the early part of the evening. She draped it across the boy in&nbsp;graceful folds, and in a way to conceal his black, conventional evening dress.&nbsp;He did not seem to mind what she did to him, only smiled, showing a faint&nbsp;gleam of white teeth, while he continued to gaze with narrowing eyes at the&nbsp;light through his glass of champagne.
“Oh! to be able to paint in color rather than in words!” exclaimed Miss Mayblunt,&nbsp;losing herself in a rhapsodic dream as she looked at him.
