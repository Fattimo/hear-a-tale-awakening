There was something extremely gorgeous about the appearance of the table,&nbsp;an effect of splendor conveyed by a cover of pale yellow satin under strips&nbsp;of lace-work. There were wax candles, in massive brass candelabra, burning&nbsp;softly under yellow silk shades; full, fragrant roses, yellow and red, abounded.&nbsp;There were silver and gold, as she had said there would be, and crystal which&nbsp;glittered like the gems which the women wore.
The ordinary stiff dining chairs had been discarded for the occasion and replaced&nbsp;by the most commodious and luxurious which could be collected throughout the&nbsp;house. Mademoiselle Reisz, being exceedingly diminutive, was elevated upon&nbsp;cushions, as small children are sometimes hoisted at table upon bulky volumes.
