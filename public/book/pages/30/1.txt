Though Edna had spoken of the dinner as a very grand affair, it was in truth&nbsp;a very small affair and very select, in so much as the guests invited were&nbsp;few and were selected with discrimination. She had counted upon an even dozen&nbsp;seating themselves at her round mahogany board, forgetting for the moment&nbsp;that Madame Ratignolle was to the last degree souffrante and unpresentable,&nbsp;and not foreseeing that Madame Lebrun would send a thousand regrets at the&nbsp;last moment. So there were only ten, after all, which made a cozy, comfortable&nbsp;number.
There were Mr. and Mrs. Merriman, a pretty, vivacious little woman in the&nbsp;thirties; her husband, a jovial fellow, something of a shallow-pate, who laughed&nbsp;a good deal at other people’s witticisms, and had thereby made himself extremely&nbsp;popular.
