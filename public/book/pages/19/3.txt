     Her husband let her alone as she requested, and went away to his office.
Edna went up to her atelier—a bright room in the top of the house. She was
working with great energy and interest, without accomplishing anything, however,
which satisfied her even in the smallest degree. For a time she had the whole
household enrolled in the service of art. The boys posed for her. They thought
it amusing at first, but the occupation soon lost its attractiveness when
they discovered that it was not a game arranged especially for their entertainment.
The quadroon sat for hours before Edna’s palette, patient as a savage, while
the house-maid took charge of the children, and the drawing-room went undusted.
