She could see the glint of the moon upon the bay, and could&nbsp;feel the soft, gusty beating of the hot south wind. A subtle current of desire&nbsp;passed through her body, weakening her hold upon the brushes and making her&nbsp;eyes burn.
There were days when she was very happy without knowing why. She was happy&nbsp;to be alive and breathing, when her whole being seemed to be one with the&nbsp;sunlight, the color, the odors, the luxuriant warmth of some perfect Southern&nbsp;day.
