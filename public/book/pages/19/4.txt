     It moved her with recollections. She could hear again the ripple of the
water, the flapping sail. She could see the glint of the moon upon the bay,
and could feel the soft, gusty beating of the hot south wind. A subtle current
of desire passed through her body, weakening her hold upon the brushes and
making her eyes burn.
     There were days when she was very happy without knowing why. She was
happy to be alive and breathing, when her whole being seemed to be one with
the sunlight, the color, the odors, the luxuriant warmth of some perfect Southern
