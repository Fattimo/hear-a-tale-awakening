 she might even invent a pretext now for going. But Edna did not go. With an
inward agony, with a flaming, outspoken revolt against the ways of Nature,
she witnessed the scene of torture.

     She was still stunned and speechless with emotion when later she leaned
over her friend to kiss her and softly say good-by. Adèle, pressing her cheek,
whispered in an exhausted voice: “Think of the children, Edna. Oh think of
the children! Remember them!”
