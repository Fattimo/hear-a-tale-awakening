She slept but a few hours. They were troubled and feverish hours, disturbed&nbsp;with dreams that were intangible, that eluded her, leaving only an impression&nbsp;upon her half-awakened senses of something unattainable. She was up and dressed&nbsp;in the cool of the early morning. The air was invigorating and steadied somewhat&nbsp;her faculties. However, she was not seeking refreshment or help from any source,&nbsp;either external or from within. She was blindly following whatever impulse&nbsp;moved her, as if she had placed herself in alien hands for direction, and&nbsp;freed her soul of responsibility.
Most of the people at that early hour were still in bed and asleep. A few,&nbsp;who intended to go over to the Chênière for mass, were moving about.
