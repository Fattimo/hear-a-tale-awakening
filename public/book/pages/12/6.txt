     The sun was high up and beginning to bite. The swift breeze seemed to
Edna to bury the sting of it into the pores of her face and hands. Robert
held his umbrella over her. As they went cutting sidewise through the water,
the sails bellied taut, with the wind filling and overflowing them. Old Monsieur
Farival laughed sardonically at something as he looked at the sails, and Beaudelet
swore at the old man under his breath.

     Sailing across the bay to the Chênière Caminada, Edna felt as if she
were being borne away from some anchorage which had held her fast, whose chains
had been loosening—had snapped the night before when the mystic spirit was
abroad, leaving her free to drift whithersoever she chose to set her sails.
