Robert knew the girl, and he talked to her a little in the boat. No one present&nbsp;understood what they said. Her name was Mariequita. She had a round, sly,&nbsp;piquant face and pretty black eyes. Her hands were small, and she kept them&nbsp;folded over the handle of her basket. Her feet were broad and coarse. She&nbsp;did not strive to hide them. Edna looked at her feet, and noticed the sand&nbsp;and slime between her brown toes.
Beaudelet grumbled because Mariequita was there, taking up so much room. In&nbsp;reality he was annoyed at having old Monsieur Farival, who considered himself&nbsp;the better sailor of the two. But he would not quarrel with so old a man as&nbsp;Monsieur Farival, so he quarreled with Mariequita. The girl was deprecatory&nbsp;at one moment, appealing to Robert.
