He put on his big straw hat, and taking his umbrella from the stand&nbsp;in the hall, followed the lady in black, never overtaking her.
The little negro girl who worked Madame Lebrun’s sewing-machine was sweeping&nbsp;the galleries with long, absent-minded strokes of the broom. Edna sent her&nbsp;up into the house to awaken Robert.
“Tell him I am going to the Chênière. The boat is ready; tell him to hurry.”
He had soon joined her. She had never sent for him before. She had never asked&nbsp;for him. She had never seemed to want him before. She did not appear conscious&nbsp;that she had done anything unusual in commanding his presence. He was apparently&nbsp;equally unconscious of anything extraordinary in the situation.
