She entered the hall with him during a lull in the dance. She made an awkward,&nbsp;imperious little bow as she went in. She was a homely woman, with a small&nbsp;weazened face and body and eyes that glowed. She had absolutely no taste in&nbsp;dress, and wore a batch of rusty black lace with a bunch of artificial violets&nbsp;pinned to the side of her hair.
“Ask Mrs. Pontellier what she would like to hear me play,” she requested of&nbsp;Robert. She sat perfectly still before the piano, not touching the keys, while&nbsp;Robert carried her message to Edna at the window. A general air of surprise&nbsp;and genuine satisfaction fell upon every one as they saw the pianist enter.&nbsp;There was a settling down, and a prevailing air of expectancy everywhere.&nbsp;Edna was a trifle embarrassed at being thus signaled out for the imperious&nbsp;little woman’s favor.
