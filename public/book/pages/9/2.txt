There was now an apparent disposition to relax; to&nbsp;widen the circle of confidences and give a more general tone to the conversation.
Many of the children had been permitted to sit up beyond their usual bedtime.&nbsp;A small band of them were lying on their stomachs on the floor looking at&nbsp;the colored sheets of the comic papers which Mr. Pontellier had brought down.&nbsp;The little Pontellier boys were permitting them to do so, and making their&nbsp;authority felt.
Music, dancing, and a recitation or two were the entertainments furnished,&nbsp;or rather, offered. But there was nothing systematic about the programme,&nbsp;no appearance of prearrangement nor even premeditation.
