Every light in the hall was ablaze; every lamp turned as high as it could be&nbsp;without smoking the chimney or threatening explosion. The lamps were fixed&nbsp;at intervals against the wall, encircling the whole room. Some one had gathered&nbsp;orange and lemon branches, and with these fashioned graceful festoons between.&nbsp;The dark green of the branches stood out and glistened against the white muslin&nbsp;curtains which draped the windows, and which puffed, floated, and flapped&nbsp;at the capricious will of a stiff breeze that swept up from the Gulf.
It was Saturday night a few weeks after the intimate conversation held between&nbsp;Robert and Madame Ratignolle on their way from the beach. An unusual number&nbsp;of husbands, fathers, and friends had come down to stay over Sunday; and they&nbsp;were being suitably entertained by their families, with the material help&nbsp;of Madame Lebrun.
