Every light in the hall was ablaze; every lamp turned as high as it could be&nbspwithout smoking the chimney or threatening explosion. The lamps were fixed&nbspat intervals against the wall, encircling the whole room. Some one had gathered&nbsporange and lemon branches, and with these fashioned graceful festoons between.&nbspThe dark green of the branches stood out and glistened against the white muslin&nbspcurtains which draped the windows, and which puffed, floated, and flapped&nbspat the capricious will of a stiff breeze that swept up from the Gulf.
It was Saturday night a few weeks after the intimate conversation held between&nbspRobert and Madame Ratignolle on their way from the beach. An unusual number&nbspof husbands, fathers, and friends had come down to stay over Sunday; and they&nbspwere being suitably entertained by their families, with the material help&nbspof Madame Lebrun.
