of every one present followed it with the overture to “The Poet and the Peasant.”
     “Allez vous-en! Sapristi!” shrieked the parrot outside the door. He was
the only being present who possessed sufficient candor to admit that he was
not listening to these gracious performances for the first time that summer.
Old Monsieur Farival, grandfather of the twins, grew indignant over the interruption,
and insisted upon having the bird removed and consigned to regions of darkness.
Victor Lebrun objected; and his decrees were as immutable as those of Fate.
The parrot fortunately offered no further interruption to the entertainment,
