“Well, how did you like my music?” she asked. The young woman was unable to&nbsp;answer; she pressed the hand of the pianist convulsively. Mademoiselle Reisz&nbsp;perceived her agitation and even her tears. She patted her again upon the&nbsp;shoulder as she said:
“You are the only one worth playing for. Those others? Bah!” and she went&nbsp;shuffling and sidling on down the gallery toward her room.
But she was mistaken about “those others.” Her playing had aroused a fever&nbsp;of enthusiasm. “What passion!” “What an artist!” “I have always said no one&nbsp;could play Chopin like Mademoiselle Reisz!” “That last prelude! Bon Dieu!&nbsp;It shakes a man!”
