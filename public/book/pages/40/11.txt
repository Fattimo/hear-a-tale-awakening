But death had passed her by. The voice was very clear and steady with which&nbsp;she spoke to Tante Lizette, brewing her tisane there in a corner.
“Ef you will give me one good drink tisane, Tante Lizette, I b’lieve I’m goin’&nbsp;sleep, me.”
And she did sleep; so soundly, so healthfully, that old Lizette without compunction&nbsp;stole softly away, to creep back through the moonlit fields to her own cabin&nbsp;in the new quarters.
The first touch of the cool gray morning awoke La Folle. She arose, calmly,&nbsp;as if no tempest had shaken and threatened her existence but yesterday.
