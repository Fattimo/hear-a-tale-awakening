She withdrew her arms from the tub of suds in which they had been plunged,&nbsp;dried them upon her apron, and as quickly as her trembling limbs would bear&nbsp;her, hurried to the spot whence the ominous report had come.
It was as she feared. There she found Chéri stretched upon the ground, with&nbsp;his rifle beside him. He moaned piteously:—
“I’m dead, La Folle! I’m dead! I’m gone!”
“Non, non!” she exclaimed resolutely, as she knelt beside him. “Put you’ arm&nbsp;’roun’ La Folle’s nake, Chéri. Dat’s nuttin’; dat goin’ be nuttin’.” She lifted&nbsp;him in her powerful arms.
Chéri had carried his gun muzzle-downward. He had stumbled,—he did not know&nbsp;how. He only knew that he had a ball lodged somewhere in his leg, and he thought&nbsp;that his end was at hand. Now, with his head upon the woman’s shoulder, he&nbsp;moaned and wept with pain and fright.
