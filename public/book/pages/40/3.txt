But none of them had stroked her black hand quite as Chéri did, nor rested&nbsp;their heads against her knee so confidingly, nor fallen asleep in her arms&nbsp;as he used to do. For Chéri hardly did such things now, since he had become&nbsp;the proud possessor of a gun, and had had his black curls cut off.
That summer—the summer Chéri gave La Folle two black curls tied with a knot&nbsp;of red ribbon—the water ran so low in the bayou that even the little children&nbsp;at Bellissime were able to cross it on foot, and the cattle were sent to pasture&nbsp;down by the river. La Folle was sorry when they were gone, for she loved these&nbsp;dumb companions well, and liked to feel that they were there, and to hear&nbsp;them browsing by night up to her own enclosure.
