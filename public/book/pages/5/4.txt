Mrs. Pontellier had brought her sketching materials, which she sometimes dabbled&nbsp;with in an unprofessional way. She liked the dabbling. She felt in it satisfaction&nbsp;of a kind which no other employment afforded her.
She had long wished to try herself on Madame Ratignolle. Never had that lady&nbsp;seemed a more tempting subject than at that moment, seated there like some&nbsp;sensuous Madonna, with the gleam of the fading day enriching her splendid&nbsp;color.
Robert crossed over and seated himself upon the step below Mrs. Pontellier,&nbsp;that he might watch her work.
