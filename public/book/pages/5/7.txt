The spell was soon over, and Mrs. Pontellier could not help wondering if there&nbsp;were not a little imagination responsible for its origin, for the rose tint&nbsp;had never faded from her friend’s face.
She stood watching the fair woman walk down the long line of galleries with&nbsp;the grace and majesty which queens are sometimes supposed to possess. Her&nbsp;little ones ran to meet her. Two of them clung about her white skirts, the&nbsp;third she took from its nurse and with a thousand endearments bore it along&nbsp;in her own fond, encircling arms. Though, as everybody well knew, the doctor&nbsp;had forbidden her to lift so much as a pin!
