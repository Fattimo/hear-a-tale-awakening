The youngsters came tumbling up the steps, the quadroon following at the respectful&nbspdistance which they required her to observe. Mrs. Pontellier made them carry&nbspher paints and things into the house. She sought to detain them for a little&nbsptalk and some pleasantry. But they were greatly in earnest. They had only&nbspcome to investigate the contents of the bonbon box. They accepted without&nbspmurmuring what she chose to give them, each holding out two chubby hands scoop-like,&nbspin the vain hope that they might be filled; and then away they went.
The sun was low in the west, and the breeze soft and languorous that came&nbspup from the south, charged with the seductive odor of the sea. Children freshly&nbspbefurbelowed, were gathering for their games under the oaks. Their voices&nbspwere high and penetrating.
