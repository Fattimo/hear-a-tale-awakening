The youngsters came tumbling up the steps, the quadroon following at the respectful&nbsp;distance which they required her to observe. Mrs. Pontellier made them carry&nbsp;her paints and things into the house. She sought to detain them for a little&nbsp;talk and some pleasantry. But they were greatly in earnest. They had only&nbsp;come to investigate the contents of the bonbon box. They accepted without&nbsp;murmuring what she chose to give them, each holding out two chubby hands scoop-like,&nbsp;in the vain hope that they might be filled; and then away they went.
The sun was low in the west, and the breeze soft and languorous that came&nbsp;up from the south, charged with the seductive odor of the sea. Children freshly&nbsp;befurbelowed, were gathering for their games under the oaks. Their voices&nbsp;were high and penetrating.
