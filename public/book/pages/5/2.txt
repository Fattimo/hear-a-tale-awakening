Mrs. Pontellier liked to sit and gaze at her fair companion as she might look&nbsp;upon a faultless Madonna.
“Could any one fathom the cruelty beneath that fair exterior?” murmured Robert.&nbsp;“She knew that I adored her once, and she let me adore her. It was ‘Robert,&nbsp;come; go; stand up; sit down; do this; do that; see if the baby sleeps; my&nbsp;thimble, please, that I left God knows where. Come and read Daudet to me while&nbsp;I sew.’”
“Par exemple! I never had to ask. You were always there under my feet, like&nbsp;a troublesome cat.”
“You mean like an adoring dog. And just as soon as Ratignolle appeared on&nbsp;the scene, then it was like a dog. ‘Passez! Adieu! Allez vous-en!’”
