They formed a congenial group sitting there that summer afternoon—Madame
Ratignolle sewing away, often stopping to relate a story or incident with much
expressive gesture of her perfect hands; Robert and Mrs. Pontellier sitting idle,
exchanging occasional words, glances or smiles which indicated a certain
advanced stage of intimacy and camaraderie.
He had lived in her shadow during the past month. No one thought anything of it.
Many had predicted that Robert would devote himself to Mrs. Pontellier when he
arrived. Since the age of fifteen, which was eleven years before, Robert each
summer at Grand Isle had constituted himself the devoted attendant of some fair
dame or damsel. Sometimes it was a young girl, again a widow; but as often as
not it was some interesting married woman.
