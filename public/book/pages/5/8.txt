“Oh, no,” she answered, with a tone of indecision. “I’m tired; I think not.”&nbsp;Her glance wandered from his face away toward the Gulf, whose sonorous murmur&nbsp;reached her like a loving but imperative entreaty.
“Oh, come!” he insisted. “You mustn’t miss your bath. Come on. The water must&nbsp;be delicious; it will not hurt you. Come.”
He reached up for her big, rough straw hat that hung on a peg outside the&nbsp;door, and put it on her head. They descended the steps, and walked away together&nbsp;toward the beach. The sun was low in the west and the breeze was soft and&nbsp;warm.
