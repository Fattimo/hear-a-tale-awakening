Octavie had experienced many such moments of despair, but a blessed resignation&nbsp;had never failed to follow, and it fell then upon her like a mantle and enveloped&nbsp;her.
“I shall grow old and quiet and sad like poor Aunt Tavie,” she murmured to&nbsp;herself as she folded the letter and replaced it in the secretary. Already&nbsp;she gave herself a little demure air like her Aunt Tavie. She walked with&nbsp;a slow glide in unconscious imitation of Mademoiselle Tavie whom some youthful&nbsp;affliction had robbed of earthly compensation while leaving her in possession&nbsp;of youth’s illusions.
