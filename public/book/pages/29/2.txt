Arobin found her with rolled sleeves, working in company with the house-maid&nbsp;when he looked in during the afternoon. She was splendid and robust, and had&nbsp;never appeared handsomer than in the old blue gown, with a red silk handkerchief&nbsp;knotted at random around her head to protect her hair from the dust. She was&nbsp;mounted upon a high stepladder, unhooking a picture from the wall when he&nbsp;entered. He had found the front door open, and had followed his ring by walking&nbsp;in unceremoniously.
“Come down!” he said. “Do you want to kill yourself?” She greeted him with&nbsp;affected carelessness, and appeared absorbed in her occupation.
If he had expected to find her languishing, reproachful, or indulging in sentimental&nbsp;tears, he must have been greatly surprised.
