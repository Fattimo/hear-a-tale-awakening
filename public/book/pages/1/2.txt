He stopped before the door of his own cottage, which was the fourth one from&nbsp;the main building and next to the last. Seating himself in a wicker rocker&nbsp;which was there, he once more applied himself to the task of reading the newspaper.&nbsp;The day was Sunday; the paper was a day old. The Sunday papers had not yet&nbsp;reached Grand Isle. He was already acquainted with the market reports, and&nbsp;he glanced restlessly over the editorials and bits of news which he had not&nbsp;had time to read before quitting New Orleans the day before.
Mr. Pontellier wore eye-glasses. He was a man of forty, of medium height and&nbsp;rather slender build; he stooped a little. His hair was brown and straight,&nbsp;parted on one side. His beard was neatly and closely trimmed.
