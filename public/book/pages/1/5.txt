“You are burnt beyond recognition,” he added, looking at his wife as one looks&nbsp;at a valuable piece of personal property which has suffered some damage. She&nbsp;held up her hands, strong, shapely hands, and surveyed them critically, drawing&nbsp;up her fawn sleeves above the wrists. Looking at them reminded her of her&nbsp;rings, which she had given to her husband before leaving for the beach. She&nbsp;silently reached out to him, and he, understanding, took the rings from his&nbsp;vest pocket and dropped them into her open palm. She slipped them upon her&nbsp;fingers; then clasping her knees, she looked across at Robert and began to&nbsp;laugh. The rings sparkled upon her fingers. He sent back an answering smile.
“What is it?” asked Pontellier, looking lazily and amused from one to the&nbsp;other. It was some utter nonsense; some adventure out there in the water,&nbsp;and they both tried to relate it at once.
