A green and yellow parrot, which hung in a cage outside the door, kept repeating&nbsp;over and over:
“Allez vous-en! Allez vous-en! Sapristi! That’s all right!”
He could speak a little Spanish, and also a language which nobody understood,&nbsp;unless it was the mocking-bird that hung on the other side of the door, whistling&nbsp;his fluty notes out upon the breeze with maddening persistence.
Mr. Pontellier, unable to read his newspaper with any degree of comfort, arose&nbsp;with an expression and an exclamation of disgust.
He walked down the gallery and across the narrow “bridges” which connected&nbsp;the Lebrun cottages one with the other. He had been seated before the door&nbsp;of the main house. The parrot and the mocking-bird were the property of Madame&nbsp;Lebrun, and they had the right to make all the noise they wished.
