Two young girls, the Farival twins,&nbsp;were playing a duet from “Zampa” upon the piano. Madame Lebrun was bustling&nbsp;in and out, giving orders in a high key to a yard-boy whenever she got inside&nbsp;the house, and directions in an equally high voice to a dining-room servant&nbsp;whenever she got outside. She was a fresh, pretty woman, clad always in white&nbsp;with elbow sleeves. Her starched skirts crinkled as she came and went. Farther&nbsp;down, before one of the cottages, a lady in black was walking demurely up&nbsp;and down, telling her beads. A good many persons of the pension had gone over&nbsp;to the Chênière Caminada in Beaudelet’s lugger to hear mass. Some young people&nbsp;were out under the water-oaks playing croquet.
