His wife stood smiling and waving, the boys&nbsp;shouting, as he disappeared in the old rockaway down the sandy road.
A few days later a box arrived for Mrs. Pontellier from New Orleans. It was&nbsp;from her husband. It was filled with friandises, with luscious and toothsome&nbsp;bits—the finest of fruits, patés, a rare bottle or two, delicious syrups,&nbsp;and bonbons in abundance.
Mrs. Pontellier was always very generous with the contents of such a box;&nbsp;she was quite used to receiving them when away from home. The patés and fruit&nbsp;were brought to the dining-room; the bonbons were passed around.
