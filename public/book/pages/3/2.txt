The result of his investigation was far from satisfactory.&nbsp;He turned and shifted the youngsters about in bed. One of them began to kick&nbsp;and talk about a basket full of crabs.
Mr. Pontellier returned to his wife with the information that Raoul had a&nbsp;high fever and needed looking after. Then he lit a cigar and went and sat&nbsp;near the open door to smoke it.
Mrs. Pontellier was quite sure Raoul had no fever. He had gone to bed perfectly&nbsp;well, she said, and nothing had ailed him all day. Mr. Pontellier was too&nbsp;well acquainted with fever symptoms to be mistaken. He assured her the child&nbsp;was consuming at that moment in the next room.
