He turned and shifted the youngsters about in bed. One of them began to kick
and talk about a basket full of crabs.

     Mr. Pontellier returned to his wife with the information that Raoul had
a high fever and needed looking after. Then he lit a cigar and went and sat
near the open door to smoke it.

     Mrs. Pontellier was quite sure Raoul had no fever. He had gone to bed
perfectly well, she said, and nothing had ailed him all day. Mr. Pontellier
was too well acquainted with fever symptoms to be mistaken. He assured her
the child was consuming at that moment in the next room.
