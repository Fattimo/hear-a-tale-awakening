following morning Mr. Pontellier was up in good time to take the rockaway which&nbsp;was to convey him to the steamer at the wharf. He was returning to the city&nbsp;to his business, and they would not see him again at the Island till the coming&nbsp;Saturday. He had regained his composure, which seemed to have been somewhat&nbsp;impaired the night before. He was eager to be gone, as he looked forward to&nbsp;a lively week in Carondelet Street.
Mr. Pontellier gave his wife half of the money which he had brought away from&nbsp;Klein’s hotel the evening before. She liked money as well as most women, and&nbsp;accepted it with no little satisfaction.
“It will buy a handsome wedding present for Sister Janet!” she exclaimed,&nbsp;smoothing out the bills as she counted them one by one.
“Oh! we’ll treat Sister Janet better than that, my dear,” he laughed, as he&nbsp;prepared to kiss her good-by.
