An indescribable oppression, which seemed to generate in some unfamiliar part&nbsp;of her consciousness, filled her whole being with a vague anguish. It was&nbsp;like a shadow, like a mist passing across her soul’s summer day. It was strange&nbsp;and unfamiliar; it was a mood. She did not sit there inwardly upbraiding her&nbsp;husband, lamenting at Fate, which had directed her footsteps to the path which&nbsp;they had taken. She was just having a good cry all to herself. The mosquitoes&nbsp;made merry over her, biting her firm, round arms and nipping at her bare insteps.
The little stinging, buzzing imps succeeded in dispelling a mood which might&nbsp;have held her there in the darkness half a night longer.
