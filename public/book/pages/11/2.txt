“Edna, dear, are you not coming in soon?” he asked again, this time fondly,&nbsp;with a note of entreaty.
“No; I am going to stay out here.”
“This is more than folly,” he blurted out. “I can’t permit you to stay out&nbsp;there all night. You must come in the house instantly.”
With a writhing motion she settled herself more securely in the hammock. She&nbsp;perceived that her will had blazed up, stubborn and resistant.
