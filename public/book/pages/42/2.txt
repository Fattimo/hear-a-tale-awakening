One day when she sat within its shadow, between her aunts, holding a hand&nbsp;of each, she said: “Tante Pélagie, I must tell you something, you and Tan’tante.”&nbsp;She spoke low, but clearly and firmly. “I love you both,—please remember that&nbsp;I love you both. But I must go away from you. I can’t live any longer here&nbsp;at Côte Joyeuse.”
A spasm passed through Mam’selle Pauline’s delicate frame. La Petite could&nbsp;feel the twitch of it in the wiry fingers that were intertwined with her own.&nbsp;Ma’ame Pélagie remained unchanged and motionless. No human eye could penetrate&nbsp;so deep as to see the satisfaction which her soul felt. She said: “What do&nbsp;you mean, Petite? Your father has sent you to us, and I am sure it is his&nbsp;wish that you remain.”
