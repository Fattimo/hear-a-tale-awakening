Ma’ame Pélagie had been sitting beside the bed in her peignoir and slippers.&nbsp;She held the hand of her sister who lay there, and smoothed down the woman’s&nbsp;soft brown hair. She said not a word, and the silence was broken only by Mam’selle&nbsp;Pauline’s continued sobs. Once Ma’ame Pélagie arose to mix a drink of orange-flower&nbsp;water, which she gave to her sister, as she would have offered it to a nervous,&nbsp;fretful child. Almost an hour passed before Ma’ame Pélagie spoke again. Then&nbsp;she said:—
“Pauline, you must cease that sobbing, now, and sleep. You will make yourself&nbsp;ill. La Petite will not go away. Do you hear me? Do you understand? She will&nbsp;stay, I promise you.”
