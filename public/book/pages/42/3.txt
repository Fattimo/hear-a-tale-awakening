I must live another life; the life I lived before. I want to know things&nbsp;that are happening from day to day over the world, and hear them talked about.&nbsp;I want my music, my books, my companions. If I had known no other life but&nbsp;this one of privation, I suppose it would be different. If I had to live this&nbsp;life, I should make the best of it. But I do not have to; and you know, tante&nbsp;Pélagie, you do not need to. It seems to me,” she added in a whisper, “that&nbsp;it is a sin against myself. Ah, Tan’tante!—what is the matter with Tan’tante?”
It was nothing; only a slight feeling of faintness, that would soon pass.&nbsp;She entreated them to take no notice; but they brought her some water and&nbsp;fanned her with a palmetto leaf.
