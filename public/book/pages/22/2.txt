“Ah, Pontellier! Not sick, I hope. Come and have a seat. What news do you bring
this morning?” He was quite portly, with a profusion of gray hair, and small blue
eyes which age had robbed of much of their brightness but none of their
penetration.
“Oh! I’m never sick, Doctor. You know that I come of tough fiber—of that old Creole
race of Pontelliers that dry up and finally blow away. I came to consult—no, not
precisely to consult—to talk to you about Edna. I don’t know what ails her.”
“Madame Pontellier not well,” marveled the Doctor. “Why, I saw her—I think it was
a week ago—walking along Canal Street, the picture of health, it seemed to me.”
