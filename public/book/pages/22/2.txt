“Ah, Pontellier! Not sick, I hope. Come and have a seat. What news do you&nbsp;bring this morning?” He was quite portly, with a profusion of gray hair, and&nbsp;small blue eyes which age had robbed of much of their brightness but none&nbsp;of their penetration.
“Oh! I’m never sick, Doctor. You know that I come of tough fiber—of that old&nbsp;Creole race of Pontelliers that dry up and finally blow away. I came to consult—no,&nbsp;not precisely to consult—to talk to you about Edna. I don’t know what ails&nbsp;her.”
“Madame Pontellier not well,” marveled the Doctor. “Why, I saw her—I think&nbsp;it was a week ago—walking along Canal Street, the picture of health, it seemed&nbsp;to me.”
