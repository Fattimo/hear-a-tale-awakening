“Do you know we have been together the whole livelong day, Robert—since early&nbsp;this morning?” she said at parting.
“All but the hundred years when you were sleeping. Good-night.”
He pressed her hand and went away in the direction of the beach. He did not&nbsp;join any of the others, but walked alone toward the Gulf.
Edna stayed outside, awaiting her husband’s return. She had no desire to sleep&nbsp;or to retire; nor did she feel like going over to sit with the Ratignolles,&nbsp;or to join Madame Lebrun and a group whose animated voices reached her as&nbsp;they sat in conversation before the house. She let her mind wander back over&nbsp;her stay at Grand Isle; and she tried to discover wherein this summer had&nbsp;been different from any and every other summer of her life.
