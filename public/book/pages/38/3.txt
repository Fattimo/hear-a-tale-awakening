“Yes,” she said. “The years that are gone seem like dreams—if one might go&nbsp;on sleeping and dreaming—but to wake up and find—oh! well! perhaps it is better&nbsp;to wake up after all, even to suffer, rather than to remain a dupe to illusions&nbsp;all one’s life.”
“It seems to me, my dear child,” said the Doctor at parting, holding her hand,&nbsp;“you seem to me to be in trouble. I am not going to ask for your confidence.&nbsp;I will only say that if ever you feel moved to give it to me, perhaps I might&nbsp;help you. I know I would understand. And I tell you there are not many who&nbsp;would—not many, my dear.”
