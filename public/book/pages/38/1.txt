Edna still felt dazed when she got outside in the open air. The Doctor’s coupé&nbsp;had returned for him and stood before the porte cochère. She did not wish&nbsp;to enter the coupé, and told Doctor Mandelet she would walk; she was not afraid,&nbsp;and would go alone. He directed his carriage to meet him at Mrs. Pontellier’s,&nbsp;and he started to walk home with her.
Up—away up, over the narrow street between the tall houses, the stars were&nbsp;blazing. The air was mild and caressing, but cool with the breath of spring&nbsp;and the night. They walked slowly, the Doctor with a heavy, measured tread&nbsp;and his hands behind him; Edna, in an absent-minded way, as she had walked&nbsp;one night at Grand Isle, as if her thoughts had gone ahead of her and she&nbsp;was striving to overtake them.
