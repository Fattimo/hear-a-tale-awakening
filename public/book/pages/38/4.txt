That is wanting&nbsp;a good deal, of course, when you have to trample upon the lives, the hearts,&nbsp;the prejudices of others—but no matter—still, I shouldn’t want to trample&nbsp;upon the little lives. “Yes, I will blame you if you don’t come and see me soon. We will talk of&nbsp;things you never have dreamt of talking about before. It will do us both good.&nbsp;I don’t want you to blame yourself, whatever comes. Good night, my child.”
She let herself in at the gate, but instead of entering she sat upon the step&nbsp;of the porch. The night was quiet and soothing. All the tearing emotion of&nbsp;the last few hours seemed to fall away from her like a somber, uncomfortable&nbsp;garment, which she had but to loosen to be rid of.
