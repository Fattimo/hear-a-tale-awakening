The people walked in little groups toward the beach. They talked and laughed;&nbsp;some of them sang. There was a band playing down at Klein’s hotel, and the&nbsp;strains reached them faintly, tempered by the distance. There were strange,&nbsp;rare odors abroad—a tangle of the sea smell and of weeds and damp, new-plowed&nbsp;earth, mingled with the heavy perfume of a field of white blossoms somewhere&nbsp;near. But the night sat lightly upon the sea and the land. There was no weight&nbsp;of darkness; there were no shadows. The white light of the moon had fallen&nbsp;upon the world like the mystery and the softness of sleep.
