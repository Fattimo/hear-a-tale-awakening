“What do you want to do?” he asked, throwing aside the paper. “Do you want&nbsp;to go out for a walk or a drive or anything? It would be a fine night to drive.”
“No; I don’t want to do anything but just be quiet. You go away and amuse&nbsp;yourself. Don’t stay.”
“I’ll go away if I must; but I shan’t amuse myself. You know that I only live&nbsp;when I am near you.”
He stood up to bid her good night.
“Is that one of the things you always say to women?”
“I have said it before, but I don’t think I ever came so near meaning it,”&nbsp;he answered with a smile. There were no warm lights in her eyes; only a dreamy,&nbsp;absent look.
“Good night. I adore you. Sleep well,” he said, and he kissed her hand and&nbsp;went away.
