The dining-room was very small. Edna’s round mahogany would have almost filled&nbsp;it. As it was there was but a step or two from the little table to the kitchen,&nbsp;to the mantel, the small buffet, and the side door that opened out on the&nbsp;narrow brick-paved yard.
A certain degree of ceremony settled upon them with the announcement of dinner.&nbsp;There was no return to personalities. Robert related incidents of his sojourn&nbsp;in Mexico, and Edna talked of events likely to interest him, which had occurred&nbsp;during his absence. The dinner was of ordinary quality, except for the few&nbsp;delicacies which she had sent out to purchase. Old Celestine, with a bandana tignon twisted&nbsp;about her head, hobbled in and out, taking a personal interest in everything;&nbsp;and she lingered occasionally to talk patois with Robert, whom she had known&nbsp;as a boy.
