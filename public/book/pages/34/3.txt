“What was she like—the one who gave you the pouch? You must have known her&nbsp;very well.”
“She was very ordinary. She wasn’t of the slightest importance. I knew her&nbsp;well enough.”
“Did you visit at her house? Was it interesting? I should like to know and&nbsp;hear about the people you met, and the impressions they made on you.”
“There are some people who leave impressions not so lasting as the imprint&nbsp;of an oar upon the water.”
“Was she such a one?”
“It would be ungenerous for me to admit that she was of that order and kind.”&nbsp;He thrust the pouch back in his pocket, as if to put away the subject with&nbsp;the trifle which had brought it up.
