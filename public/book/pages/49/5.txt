“They are children playing a game,” thought he. “I shall know more about it&nbsp;if I watch long enough.”
At the approach of night they had all vanished away with their din and smoke.&nbsp;Then the old bird plumed his feathers. At last he had understood! With a flap&nbsp;of his great, black wings he shot downward, circling toward the plain.
A man was picking his way across the plain. He was dressed in the garb of&nbsp;a clergyman. His mission was to administer the consolations of religion to&nbsp;any of the prostrate figures in whom there might yet linger a spark of life.&nbsp;A negro accompanied him, bearing a bucket of water and a flask of wine.
There were no wounded here; they had been borne away. But the retreat had&nbsp;been hurried and the vultures and the good Samaritans would have to look to&nbsp;the dead.
