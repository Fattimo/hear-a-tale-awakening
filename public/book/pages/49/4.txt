Then his dream was clamor.
     “Git your duds! you! Frenchy!” Nick was bellowing in his face. There
was what appeared to be a scramble and a rush rather than any regulated movement.
The hill side was alive with clatter and motion; with sudden up-springing
lights among the pines. In the east the dawn was unfolding out of the darkness.
Its glimmer was yet dim in the plain below.
     “What’s it all about?” wondered a big black bird perched in the top of
the tallest tree. He was an old solitary and a wise one, yet he was not wise
enough to guess what it was all about. So all day long he kept blinking and
wondering.
