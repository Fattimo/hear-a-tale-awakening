One night in autumn a few men were gathered about a fire on the slope of a&nbsp;hill. They belonged to a small detachment of Confederate forces and were awaiting&nbsp;orders to march. Their gray uniforms were worn beyond the point of shabbiness.&nbsp;One of the men was heating something in a tin cup over the embers. Two were&nbsp;lying at full length a little distance away, while a fourth was trying to&nbsp;decipher a letter and had drawn close to the light. He had unfastened his&nbsp;collar and a good bit of his flannel shirt front.
“What’s that you got around your neck, Ned?” asked one of the men lying in&nbsp;the obscurity.
Ned—or Edmond—mechanically fastened another button of his shirt and did not&nbsp;reply. He went on reading his letter.
