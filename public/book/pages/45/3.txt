He ordered the corbeille from Paris, and contained himself with&nbsp;what patience he could until it arrived; then they were married.
Madame Valmondé had not seen Désirée and the baby for four weeks. When she&nbsp;reached L’Abri she shuddered at the first sight of it, as she always did.&nbsp;It was a sad looking place, which for many years had not known the gentle&nbsp;presence of a mistress, old Monsieur Aubigny having married and buried his&nbsp;wife in France, and she having loved her own land too well ever to leave it.&nbsp;The roof came down steep and black like a cowl, reaching out beyond the wide&nbsp;galleries that encircled the yellow stuccoed house.
