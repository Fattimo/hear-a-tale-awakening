It was an October afternoon; the sun was just sinking. Out in the still fields&nbsp;the negroes were picking cotton.
Désirée had not changed the thin white garment nor the slippers which she&nbsp;wore. Her hair was uncovered and the sun’s rays brought a golden gleam from&nbsp;its brown meshes. She did not take the broad, beaten road which led to the&nbsp;far-off plantation of Valmondé. She walked across a deserted field, where&nbsp;the stubble bruised her tender feet, so delicately shod, and tore her thin&nbsp;gown to shreds.
She disappeared among the reeds and willows that grew thick along the banks&nbsp;of the deep, sluggish bayou; and she did not come back again.
