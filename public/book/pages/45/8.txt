One&nbsp;of La Blanche’s little quadroon boys—half naked too—stood fanning the child&nbsp;slowly with a fan of peacock feathers. Désirée’s eyes had been fixed absently&nbsp;and sadly upon the baby, while she was striving to penetrate the threatening&nbsp;mist that she felt closing about her. She looked from her child to the boy&nbsp;who stood beside him, and back again; over and over. “Ah!” It was a cry that&nbsp;she could not help; which she was not conscious of having uttered. The blood&nbsp;turned like ice in her veins, and a clammy moisture gathered upon her face.
She tried to speak to the little quadroon boy; but no sound would come, at&nbsp;first. When he heard his name uttered, he looked up, and his mistress was&nbsp;pointing to the door. He laid aside the great, soft fan, and obediently stole&nbsp;away, over the polished floor, on his bare tiptoes.
