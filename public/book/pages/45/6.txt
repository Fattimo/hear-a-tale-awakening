Even Négrillon, who pretended to have burnt his&nbsp;leg that he might rest from work—he only laughed, and said Négrillon was a&nbsp;great scamp. Oh, mamma, I’m so happy; it frightens me.”
What Désirée said was true. Marriage, and later the birth of his son had softened&nbsp;Armand Aubigny’s imperious and exacting nature greatly. This was what made&nbsp;the gentle Désirée so happy, for she loved him desperately. When he frowned&nbsp;she trembled, but loved him. When he smiled, she asked no greater blessing&nbsp;of God. But Armand’s dark, handsome face had not often been disfigured by&nbsp;frowns since the day he fell in love with her.
