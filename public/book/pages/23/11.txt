his old-fashioned cloak across his breast as he strode home through the darkness.
He knew his fellow-creatures better than most men; knew that inner life which
so seldom unfolds itself to unanointed eyes. He was sorry he had accepted
Pontellier’s invitation. He was growing old, and beginning to need rest and
an imperturbed spirit. He did not want the secrets of other lives thrust upon
him.

     “I hope it isn’t Arobin,” he muttered to himself as he walked. “I hope
to heaven it isn’t Alcée Arobin.”
