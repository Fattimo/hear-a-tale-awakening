The Colonel, with little sense of humor and of the fitness of things, related&nbsp;a somber episode of those dark and bitter days, in which he had acted a conspicuous&nbsp;part and always formed a central figure. Nor was the Doctor happier in his&nbsp;selection, when he told the old, ever new and curious story of the waning&nbsp;of a woman’s love, seeking strange, new channels, only to return to its legitimate&nbsp;source after days of fierce unrest. It was one of the many little human documents&nbsp;which had been unfolded to him during his long career as a physician. The&nbsp;story did not seem especially to impress Edna.
