He had certain recollections of racing in what he called&nbsp;“the good old times” when the Lecompte stables flourished, and he drew upon&nbsp;this fund of memories so that he might not be left out and seem wholly devoid&nbsp;of the modern spirit. But he failed to impose upon the Colonel, and was even&nbsp;far from impressing him with this trumped-up knowledge of bygone days. Edna&nbsp;had staked her father on his last venture, with the most gratifying results&nbsp;to both of them. Besides, they had met some very charming people, according&nbsp;to the Colonel’s impressions. Mrs. Mortimer Merriman and Mrs. James Highcamp,&nbsp;who were there with Alcée Arobin, had joined them and had enlivened the hours&nbsp;in a fashion that warmed him to think of.
