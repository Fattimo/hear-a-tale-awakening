The Colonel drank numerous “toddies” during the course of the day, which left&nbsp;him, however, imperturbed. He was an expert at concocting strong drinks. He&nbsp;had even invented some, to which he had given fantastic names, and for whose&nbsp;manufacture he required diverse ingredients that it devolved upon Edna to&nbsp;procure for him.
When Doctor Mandelet dined with the Pontelliers on Thursday he could discern&nbsp;in Mrs. Pontellier no trace of that morbid condition which her husband had&nbsp;reported to him. She was excited and in a manner radiant. She and her father&nbsp;had been to the race course, and their thoughts when they seated themselves&nbsp;at table were still occupied with the events of the afternoon, and their talk&nbsp;was still of the track.
