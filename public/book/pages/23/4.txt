There were one or two men whom she observed at the soirée musicale; but she&nbsp;would never have felt moved to any kittenish display to attract their notice—to&nbsp;any feline or feminine wiles to express herself toward them. Their personality&nbsp;attracted her in an agreeable way. Her fancy selected them, and she was glad&nbsp;when a lull in the music gave them an opportunity to meet her and talk with&nbsp;her. Often on the street the glance of strange eyes had lingered in her memory,&nbsp;and sometimes had disturbed her.
