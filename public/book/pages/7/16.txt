Edna did not reveal so much as all this to Madame Ratignolle that summer day&nbsp;when they sat with faces turned to the sea. But a good part of it escaped&nbsp;her. She had put her head down on Madame Ratignolle’s shoulder. She was flushed&nbsp;and felt intoxicated with the sound of her own voice and the unaccustomed&nbsp;taste of candor. It muddled her like wine, or like a first breath of freedom.
There was the sound of approaching voices. It was Robert, surrounded by a&nbsp;troop of children, searching for them. The two little Pontelliers were with&nbsp;him, and he carried Madame Ratignolle’s little girl in his arms. There were&nbsp;other children beside, and two nurse-maids followed, looking disagreeable&nbsp;and resigned.
