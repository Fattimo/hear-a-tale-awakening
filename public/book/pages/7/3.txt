The women were both of goodly height, Madame Ratignolle possessing the more&nbsp;feminine and matronly figure. The charm of Edna Pontellier’s physique stole&nbsp;insensibly upon you. The lines of her body were long, clean and symmetrical;&nbsp;it was a body which occasionally fell into splendid poses; there was no suggestion&nbsp;of the trim, stereotyped fashion-plate about it. A casual and indiscriminating&nbsp;observer, in passing, might not cast a second glance upon the figure. But&nbsp;with more feeling and discernment he would have recognized the noble beauty&nbsp;of its modeling, and the graceful severity of poise and movement, which made&nbsp;Edna Pontellier different from the crowd.
