She was a grown young woman when she was overtaken by what she supposed to&nbsp;be the climax of her fate. It was when the face and figure of a great tragedian&nbsp;began to haunt her imagination and stir her senses. The persistence of the&nbsp;infatuation lent it an aspect of genuineness. The hopelessness of it colored&nbsp;it with the lofty tones of a great passion.
The picture of the tragedian stood enframed upon her desk. Any one may possess&nbsp;the portrait of a tragedian without exciting suspicion or comment. (This was&nbsp;a sinister reflection which she cherished.) In the presence of others she&nbsp;expressed admiration for his exalted gifts, as she handed the photograph around&nbsp;and dwelt upon the fidelity of the likeness. When alone she sometimes picked&nbsp;it up and kissed the cold glass passionately.
