“Likely as not it was Sunday,” she laughed; “and I was running away from prayers,&nbsp;from the Presbyterian service, read in a spirit of gloom by my father that&nbsp;chills me yet to think of.”
“And have you been running away from prayers ever since, ma chère?” asked&nbsp;Madame Ratignolle, amused.
“No! oh, no!” Edna hastened to say. “I was a little unthinking child in those&nbsp;days, just following a misleading impulse without question. On the contrary,&nbsp;during one period of my life religion took a firm hold upon me; after I was&nbsp;twelve and until—until—why, I suppose until now, though I never thought much&nbsp;about it—just driven along by habit.
