“Likely as not it was Sunday,” she laughed; “and I was running away from prayers,&nbspfrom the Presbyterian service, read in a spirit of gloom by my father that&nbspchills me yet to think of.”
“And have you been running away from prayers ever since, ma chère?” asked&nbspMadame Ratignolle, amused.
“No! oh, no!” Edna hastened to say. “I was a little unthinking child in those&nbspdays, just following a misleading impulse without question.
