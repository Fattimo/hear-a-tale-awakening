Mrs. Pontellier had brought down her key through force of habit. Unlocking&nbsp;the door of her bath-room she went inside, and soon emerged, bringing a rug,&nbsp;which she spread upon the floor of the gallery, and two huge hair pillows&nbsp;covered with crash, which she placed against the front of the building.
The two seated themselves there in the shade of the porch, side by side, with&nbsp;their backs against the pillows and their feet extended. Madame Ratignolle&nbsp;removed her veil, wiped her face with a rather delicate handkerchief, and&nbsp;fanned herself with the fan which she always carried suspended somewhere about&nbsp;her person by a long, narrow ribbon.
