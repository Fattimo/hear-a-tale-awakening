All her years she had squatted and waddled there upon&nbsp;the island, gathering legends of the Baratarians and the sea. The night came&nbsp;on, with the moon to lighten it. Edna could hear the whispering voices of&nbsp;dead men and the click of muffled gold.
When she and Robert stepped into Tonie’s boat, with the red lateen sail, misty&nbsp;spirit forms were prowling in the shadows and among the reeds, and upon the&nbsp;water were phantom ships, speeding to cover.
