“You have slept precisely one hundred years. I was left here to guard your&nbsp;slumbers; and for one hundred years I have been out under the shed reading&nbsp;a book. The only evil I couldn’t prevent was to keep a broiled fowl from drying&nbsp;up.”
“If it has turned to stone, still will I eat it,” said Edna, moving with him&nbsp;into the house. “But really, what has become of Monsieur Farival and the others?”
“Gone hours ago. When they found that you were sleeping they thought it best&nbsp;not to awake you. Any way, I wouldn’t have let them. What was I here for?”
