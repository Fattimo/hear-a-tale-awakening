They waited a good while under the orange trees, till Madame Antoine came&nbsp;back, panting, waddling, with a thousand apologies to explain her absence.&nbsp;Tonie did not dare to return. He was shy, and would not willingly face any&nbsp;woman except his mother.
It was very pleasant to stay there under the orange trees, while the sun dipped&nbsp;lower and lower, turning the western sky to flaming copper and gold. The shadows&nbsp;lengthened and crept out like stealthy, grotesque monsters across the grass.
Edna and Robert both sat upon the ground—that is, he lay upon the ground beside&nbsp;her, occasionally picking at the hem of her muslin gown.
Madame Antoine seated her fat body, broad and squat, upon a bench beside the&nbsp;door. She had been talking all the afternoon, and had wound herself up to&nbsp;the storytelling pitch.
