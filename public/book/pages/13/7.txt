When she had completed her toilet she walked into the adjoining room. She was
very hungry. No one was there. But there was a cloth spread upon the table that
stood against the wall, and a cover was laid for one, with a crusty brown loaf and a
bottle of wine beside the plate. Edna bit a piece from the brown loaf, tearing it with
her strong, white teeth. She poured some of the wine into the glass and drank it
down. Then she went softly out of doors, and plucking an orange from the
low-hanging bough of a tree, threw it at Robert, who did not know she was
awake and up.
An illumination broke over his whole face when he saw her and joined her under
the orange tree.
