When she had completed her toilet she walked into the adjoining room. She&nbsp;was very hungry. No one was there. But there was a cloth spread upon the table&nbsp;that stood against the wall, and a cover was laid for one, with a crusty brown&nbsp;loaf and a bottle of wine beside the plate. Edna bit a piece from the brown&nbsp;loaf, tearing it with her strong, white teeth. She poured some of the wine&nbsp;into the glass and drank it down. Then she went softly out of doors, and plucking&nbsp;an orange from the low-hanging bough of a tree, threw it at Robert, who did&nbsp;not know she was awake and up.
An illumination broke over his whole face when he saw her and joined her under&nbsp;the orange tree.
