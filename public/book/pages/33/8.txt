So he had come back because the Mexicans were not congenial; because business&nbsp;was as profitable here as there; because of any reason, and not because he&nbsp;cared to be near her. She remembered the day she sat on the floor, turning&nbsp;the pages of his letter, seeking the reason which was left untold.
She had not noticed how he looked—only feeling his presence; but she turned&nbsp;deliberately and observed him. After all, he had been absent but a few months,&nbsp;and was not changed. His hair—the color of hers—waved back from his temples&nbsp;in the same way as before. His skin was not more burned than it had been at&nbsp;Grand Isle.
