A hundred times Edna had pictured Robert’s return, and imagined their first&nbsp;meeting. It was usually at her home, whither he had sought her out at once.&nbsp;She always fancied him expressing or betraying in some way his love for her.&nbsp;And here, the reality was that they sat ten feet apart, she at the window,&nbsp;crushing geranium leaves in her hand and smelling them, he twirling around&nbsp;on the piano stool, saying:
“I was very much surprised to hear of Mr. Pontellier’s absence; it’s a wonder&nbsp;Mademoiselle Reisz did not tell me; and your moving—mother told me yesterday.&nbsp;I should think you would have gone to New York with him, or to Iberville with&nbsp;the children, rather than be bothered here with housekeeping. And you are&nbsp;going abroad, too, I hear.
