“Come in,” she called, turning her face toward the door. And this time it&nbsp;was Robert Lebrun who presented himself. She attempted to rise; she could&nbsp;not have done so without betraying the agitation which mastered her at sight&nbsp;of him, so she fell back upon the stool, only exclaiming, “Why, Robert!”
He came and clasped her hand, seemingly without knowing what he was saying&nbsp;or doing.
“Mrs. Pontellier! How do you happen—oh! how well you look! Is Mademoiselle&nbsp;Reisz not here? I never expected to see you.”
