Edna sat at the window, which looked out over the house-tops and across the&nbsp;river. The window frame was filled with pots of flowers, and she sat and picked&nbsp;the dry leaves from a rose geranium. The day was warm, and the breeze which&nbsp;blew from the river was very pleasant. She removed her hat and laid it on&nbsp;the piano. She went on picking the leaves and digging around the plants with&nbsp;her hat pin. Once she thought she heard Mademoiselle Reisz approaching. But&nbsp;it was a young black girl, who came in, bringing a small bundle of laundry,&nbsp;which she deposited in the adjoining room, and went away.
