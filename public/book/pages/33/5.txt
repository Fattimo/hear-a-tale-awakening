     Edna sat at the window, which looked out over the house-tops and across
the river. The window frame was filled with pots of flowers, and she sat and
picked the dry leaves from a rose geranium. The day was warm, and the breeze
which blew from the river was very pleasant. She removed her hat and laid
it on the piano. She went on picking the leaves and digging around the plants
with her hat pin. Once she thought she heard Mademoiselle Reisz approaching.
But it was a young black girl, who came in, bringing a small bundle of laundry,
which she deposited in the adjoining room, and went away.
     Edna seated herself at the piano, and softly picked out with one hand
the bars of a piece of music which lay open before her. A half-hour went by.
There was the occasional sound of people going and coming in the lower hall.
