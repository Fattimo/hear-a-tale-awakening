They went together, picking their way across muddy streets and sidewalks encumbered&nbsp;with the cheap display of small tradesmen. Part of the distance they rode&nbsp;in the car, and after disembarking, passed the Pontellier mansion, which looked&nbsp;broken and half torn asunder. Robert had never known the house, and looked&nbsp;at it with interest.
“I never knew you in your home,” he remarked.
“I am glad you did not.”
“Why?” She did not answer. They went on around the corner, and it seemed as&nbsp;if her dreams were coming true after all, when he followed her into the little&nbsp;house.
“You must stay and dine with me, Robert. You see I am all alone, and it is&nbsp;so long since I have seen you. There is so much I want to ask you.”
