Besides, she was consumed&nbsp;with curiosity to see the little house and the manner in which it was conducted.&nbsp;She wanted to hear all about the dinner party; Monsieur Ratignolle had left so early.&nbsp;What had happened after he left? The champagne and grapes which Edna sent&nbsp;over were too delicious. She had so little appetite; they had refreshed and&nbsp;toned her stomach. Where on earth was she going to put Mr. Pontellier in that&nbsp;little house, and the boys? And then she made Edna promise to go to her when&nbsp;her hour of trial overtook her.
“At any time—any time of the day or night, dear,” Edna assured her.
